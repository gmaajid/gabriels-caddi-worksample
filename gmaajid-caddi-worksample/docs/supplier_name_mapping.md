## Method Comparison on This Data

| Method | Clusters Found | Correct? | Notes |
|--------|:--------------:|:--------:|-------|
| **Jaccard** | 6 | Yes | All 4 Apex variants merged correctly |
| **Embedding** | 7 | **No** | Split Apex into 2 clusters: {APEX MFG, Apex Mfg} and {APEX Manufacturing Inc, Apex Manufacturing Inc}. "MFG" is too distant from "Manufacturing" in embedding space. |
| **Hybrid** | 6 | Yes | Jaccard component compensates for embedding's abbreviation blindness |

## Impact of Normalization

Without normalization, Apex appears as 4 separate suppliers ranked 3rd-7th by order count.
With normalization, the true picture emerges:

| Rank | Supplier | Orders | % of Total | Spend Share |
|:----:|----------|:------:|:----------:|:-----------:|
| 1 | **Apex Manufacturing** | 237 | 47.4% | Dominant |
| 2 | TitanForge LLC | 60 | 12.0% | |
| 3 | AeroFlow Systems | 52 | 10.4% | |
| 4 | Stellar Metalworks | 52 | 10.4% | |
| 5 | QuickFab Industries | 50 | 10.0% | |
| 6 | Precision Thermal Co | 49 | 9.8% | |

**Key insight**: Apex Manufacturing handles nearly half of all orders. This concentration
is invisible without normalization and has major implications for supply risk assessment.

## Reproducibility

```bash
# From project root, in the virtual environment:
.venv/bin/python -c "
from rag.models import init_supplier_normalizer, get_supplier_clusters
import pandas as pd

orders = pd.read_csv('data/Copy of supplier_orders.csv')
rfq = pd.read_csv('data/Copy of rfq_responses.csv')
all_names = list(orders['supplier_name']) + list(rfq['supplier_name'])

init_supplier_normalizer(all_names)
for canonical, variants in sorted(get_supplier_clusters().items()):
    print(f'{canonical}: {variants}')
"
```
